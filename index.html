About

We help you claim what you earned.

Legacy Veterans Benefit Services exists to support veterans who feel overwhelmed by the VA disability process. Forms, evidence, timelines, and confusing letters turn an earned benefit into a source of stress.

The focus stays on clear steps, plain language, and steady support as you move through each stage of your claim or appeal.

Important: Legacy Veterans Benefit Services is a private business and not part of the Department of Veterans Affairs or any government agency. This service does not provide legal advice.

Are you part of the VA?
No. Legacy Veterans Benefit Services is a private business. Support focuses on helping you understand and respond to VA decisions, not on speaking for the VA. Think of it as having a knowledgeable guide beside you as you navigate the process.
Do you guarantee a rating or back pay?
No one controls VA outcomes. The focus remains on accurate information, strong evidence, and clear strategy to give your claim the best chance possible. Anyone who guarantees a specific rating or outcome is not being honest with you.
Do you provide legal advice?
No. The service offers education, guidance, and support — not legal representation. If your situation requires legal representation (such as a Board of Veterans Appeals hearing with a judge), you will be told that clearly and directed to appropriate resources.
Who can use this service?
Veterans, their spouses, and survivors navigating VA disability claims. Whether you are just starting, have been denied, or feel stuck at a particular stage of the process — the free consultation is the best way to find out if this service is a good fit for your situation.
How quickly will I hear back?
All messages and form submissions receive a response within one business day. For the fastest response, calling (253) 592-2250 directly is recommended.
